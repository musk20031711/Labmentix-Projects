Uber Ride Request Analysis – Identifying Peak Hour Failures¶

Project Type - Exploratory Data Analysis.¶

Contribution- Individual.¶

Project Summary- Uber Request Data – Exploratory Data Analysis.¶

This project aims to analyze Uber ride request data to identify operational inefficiencies affecting ride fulfillment. The dataset contains trip-level details, including request timestamps, pickup locations, and trip statuses such as "Completed," "Cancelled," and "No Cars Available." Through exploratory data analysis, we examined trends across time slots, pickup points, and ride statuses to uncover patterns behind trip failures.

The analysis revealed that the majority of ride cancellations occur during the morning rush hours (5–9 AM), especially for trips from the airport to the city. Meanwhile, the evening rush (5–9 PM) shows a significant rise in “No Cars Available” status, mainly for trips from the city to the airport. These patterns point to a clear mismatch between ride demand and driver supply during peak hours.

To address these issues, the project recommends dynamic driver allocation, time-based incentives, and predictive scheduling. By implementing these data-driven strategies, Uber can improve ride fulfillment rates, enhance customer satisfaction, and drive higher operational efficiency.

This EDA not only identifies key problem areas but also provides actionable insights to support better decision-making for Uber’s service optimization.

Problem Statement-¶

Uber is facing frequent ride cancellations and driver unavailability, especially during certain times of the day. This project aims to explore patterns in Uber ride request data to uncover what times, locations, and statuses lead to maximum ride failures.

Business Objective-¶

1.Identify operational inefficiencies in the Uber ride request system.

2.Analyze patterns by time, location, and ride status.

3.Suggest strategies to improve ride fulfillment and reduce cancellations or unavailability.

# Categorize time slots
def get_time_slot(hour):
    if 4 <= hour < 10:
        return 'Morning Rush'
    elif 10 <= hour < 17:
        return 'Day Time'
    elif 17 <= hour < 22:
        return 'Evening Rush'
    else:
        return 'Late Night'

df['Time Slot'] = df['Request hour'].apply(get_time_slot)

8. Business Objective Solution-¶

To help Uber achieve its business objective of improving ride fulfillment and customer satisfaction, our analysis recommends a series of strategic actions derived from the observed trends and inefficiencies in the dataset:

Optimize Driver Allocation Based on Time and Location During peak hours—especially 5–9 AM and 5–9 PM—there’s a notable mismatch between demand and supply. Uber should deploy more drivers in the Airport zone during mornings (to reduce cancellations) and in the City zone during evenings (to reduce “No Cars Available”).

Introduce Time-Specific Driver Incentives To reduce morning cancellations, especially from Airport to City, Uber can offer financial incentives or surge bonuses to motivate drivers to accept these requests.

Implement Predictive Demand Forecasting Using historical data patterns, Uber can build a predictive model to estimate demand by hour and location. This model can guide dynamic supply allocation in real time, ensuring demand fulfillment.

Driver Engagement and Training Cancellations could also be linked to driver dissatisfaction or discomfort with certain routes (e.g., long-distance airport trips). Training and feedback loops can help mitigate this.

Strengthen Customer Communication Communicating estimated wait times more transparently and offering alternatives like scheduled rides during peak times may help improve the customer experience.

Strategic Outcome:¶

By implementing these data-driven strategies, Uber can expect:

1.Higher trip completion rates.

2.Reduced cancellation and failure percentages.

3.Improved driver satisfaction.

4.Stronger customer loyalty and retention.

5.Greater revenue and operational efficiency.

6.These targeted solutions convert insights into actionable value, helping Uber bridge its supply-demand gaps and strengthen its market competitiveness.

9. Insights-¶

  1. Most requests are between 5–9 AM and 5–8 PM — Morning & Evening Rush.
  1. High number of ‘No Cars Available’ during Evening Rush from City to Airport.
  1. Most cancellations happen during Morning Rush from Airport to City.

10. Final Project Summary¶

This project explored Uber ride request data to uncover patterns causing operational inefficiencies, particularly during peak hours. Through structured exploratory data analysis, we identified that most ride failures occur due to two main issues: high cancellation rates during the morning rush hours (5–9 AM)—especially from Airport to City, and unavailability of cars during the evening rush (5–9 PM)—especially from City to Airport.

The analysis also highlighted the City zone as a high-demand area with frequent “No Cars Available” cases, while Airport pickups faced a high cancellation ratio. These patterns indicate a clear supply-demand mismatch driven by time and location.

Based on these findings, strategic solutions were proposed, including time- and location-based driver deployment, incentive programs, and predictive demand models to improve service reliability. If implemented, these recommendations can significantly enhance ride fulfillment rates, improve customer satisfaction, and optimize driver utilization, ultimately leading to stronger operational performance and business growth for Uber.

This EDA project demonstrates how data-driven decision-making can be used to solve real-world business problems in the transportation industry.
